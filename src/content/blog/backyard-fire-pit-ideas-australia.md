There's something primal about gathering around a fire. In Australia, where we live outdoors for most of the year, a well-positioned fire pit can become the heart of your backyard — the place where everyone naturally gravitates as the evening cools.

## Types of Fire Pits for Australian Backyards

### Portable Steel Fire Bowls

The most accessible option. These sit on legs, can be moved around the yard, and packed away when not needed — which makes them perfect for renters or those who like flexibility. Starting from around $80 at Bunnings, they're the entry point for most Australians.

Look for:
- Thick-gauge steel (it lasts longer and holds heat better)
- A spark guard included or available separately
- Legs that elevate the bowl safely above the ground

### Built-In Stone Fire Pits

For those committed to the space, a built-in stone or concrete fire pit is the ultimate backyard investment. Positioned at the centre of a seating area, they become a permanent focal point that adds genuine value to the property.

Materials that work well in Australian conditions:
- **Sandstone** — beautiful, warm, and very Australian
- **Bluestone** — sleek, contemporary, holds up well
- **Concrete** — industrial look, very durable

### Gas Fire Pits

No smoke, no mess, instant flame at the turn of a dial. Gas fire pits are increasingly popular in Australian backyards, particularly in areas with strict fire regulations. They're more expensive upfront but lower maintenance.

## Seating Around the Fire Pit

The seating arrangement matters as much as the fire pit itself. Go for:

- **Low-slung chairs** or Adirondack-style chairs that invite you to lean back and relax
- **A curved concrete bench** built around the fire pit for a seamless look
- **Scattered poufs and floor cushions** for a more relaxed, boho feel

Keep seating at a comfortable distance — at least 1.5 metres from the edge of the pit.

## Safety Essentials

- Always check fire ban status before lighting
- Keep a bucket of water or garden hose nearby
- Never light under a low pergola or near overhanging branches
- Let coals cool completely before leaving
