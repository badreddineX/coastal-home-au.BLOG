If there's one thing that defines Australian outdoor entertaining, it's the BBQ. It's where we gather, where the stories get told, and where the weekend actually begins. Getting your BBQ area right is the single highest-impact upgrade you can make to your outdoor space.

## Types of Outdoor BBQ Setups

### The Freestanding BBQ with a Prep Zone

The starting point for most Australians. A quality four-burner gas BBQ on wheels, positioned near a prep bench or outdoor table. Simple, flexible, and effective.

What makes it work:
- A quality freestanding BBQ (Weber Q or BeefEater are the gold standards)
- A prep bench positioned alongside — even a simple stainless steel outdoor bench from Bunnings works brilliantly
- A small outdoor bar fridge nearby for drinks and marinades

### The Built-In BBQ Kitchen

The dream for serious outdoor entertainers. A built-in outdoor kitchen integrates the BBQ, a sink, storage, and a benchtop into a permanent outdoor cooking station.

Essential elements:
- **Stone or porcelain benchtop** — granite, bluestone, or porcelain slab all handle weather beautifully
- **Built-in BBQ or pizza oven** set into the benchtop
- **Under-bench storage** in marine-grade stainless or powder-coated aluminium
- **Outdoor sink** with a cold tap at minimum (hot and cold if you're going all in)
- **Bar fridge** or wine fridge set into the cabinetry

### The Outdoor Pizza Oven Setup

Pizza ovens have exploded in popularity in Australian backyards. A wood-fired pizza oven creates an incredible social focal point and opens up a whole new world of outdoor cooking — not just pizza but bread, slow-cooked meats, and roasted vegetables.

## Layout and Positioning

Position your BBQ area:
- **Downwind** of your main seating area — nobody wants smoke in their face
- **Close to the house** for easy access to the kitchen for supplies
- **Near power** for lighting, a bar fridge, and any electric appliances
- **Under cover** — cooking in the rain is miserable, cooking under a pergola is wonderful

## The Finishing Details

- **Task lighting** directly over the cooking surface — you need to see what you're cooking
- **A good outdoor speaker** — cooking is more fun with music
- **An esky or outdoor bar fridge** within arm's reach
- **A hose connection** nearby for easy clean-up
- **Splashback** behind the BBQ in tile or stainless steel to protect the wall

## Australian BBQ Brands Worth Knowing

- **Weber** — the most trusted BBQ brand in Australia, excellent warranty and support
- **BeefEater** — Australian-owned, excellent quality, built for our conditions
- **Everdure by Heston** — premium, design-forward, genuinely beautiful BBQs
- **Matador (Bunnings)** — solid entry-level option if you're budget-conscious
