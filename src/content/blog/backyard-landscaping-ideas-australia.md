Your backyard is your most underutilised space. Most Australians have more outdoor space than they realise — they just haven't organised it into something they want to actually spend time in. Good landscaping changes that.

## Start With a Plan

Before you spend a dollar, sketch your backyard and define the zones you want:

1. **Entertaining zone** — your alfresco dining and lounging area
2. **Garden beds** — where the plants live
3. **Lawn** — if you want it (and if your climate suits it)
4. **Utility zone** — bins, the clothes line, the shed

Getting these zones clear means every landscaping decision you make will have a purpose.

## Paving and Ground Cover

The ground is the largest surface in your backyard — getting it right matters.

**Paving options for Australian backyards:**
- **Concrete pavers** — affordable, practical, can be styled up with good furniture
- **Sandstone pavers** — beautiful warm tones that suit coastal Australian homes
- **Timber decking** — brings warmth and is perfect for entertaining areas
- **Artificial turf** — low maintenance, great for shady spots or high-traffic areas with kids

Consider a combination: paving in the entertaining zone, lawn or turf in the play area, garden beds around the perimeter.

## Plants: Go Native First

Australian native plants are the smartest choice for most backyards:

- **Lomandra** — tough, grass-like, looks great as a border or mass planting
- **Westringia** — the Australian coastal rosemary, perfect for hedging
- **Grevillea** — flowering, attracts birds, hundreds of varieties
- **Agapanthus** — flowering, low water once established, beautiful blue-purple flowers

For a coastal garden feel, add:
- Coastal grasses (Poa, Themeda)
- Pigface (Carpobrotus) as groundcover
- Coastal banksia for structure

## Defining Garden Beds

A curved garden bed looks far more natural and considered than a straight-edged one. Use a garden hose to experiment with the shape before you dig. Edge it with steel garden edging for a clean, modern look, or sandstone for something more natural.

Mulch heavily — it suppresses weeds, retains moisture, and makes the whole garden look cared for with minimal effort.

## Lighting the Backyard

Good landscape lighting transforms your backyard after dark:

- **Path lights** along garden bed edges or pathways
- **Uplights** pointing up into feature trees or plants
- **String lights** across the entertaining area
- **Step lights** if you have level changes

Solar-powered options have improved enormously — Bunnings now carries excellent ranges that look good and actually work.
