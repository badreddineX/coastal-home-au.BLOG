I've been building the Hamptons living room in our Gold Coast home for three years "” gradually, as budget allowed, and with a clear picture of what I was working toward. The Australian version of Hamptons is not the formal, heavily panelled American original. It's warmer, more relaxed, and more suited to living with.

Here are the 13 specific ideas that made our living room read as genuinely Hamptons rather than just "white and navy."

![Hamptons style living room with white linen sofa, jute rug, navy cushions, and VJ panel feature wall](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1555041469-a586c61ea9bc?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

## Tip 1: Make White the Dominant Colour "” Everything Else is Secondary

In a Hamptons living room, white is not a background colour "” it is the dominant element. Walls, trim, ceiling, built-in shelving, and primary furniture should all be in the white family. The navy, the natural timber, the jute "” all of these read as accents against the white ground.

**My palette:**
- Walls: Dulux Vivid White (slightly cooler than Natural White "” suits the Hamptons look)
- Trim and doors: Dulux Vivid White semi-gloss (same colour, different finish)
- Sofa: cream linen, Freedom ($2,499)
- Built-in shelves: painted white with Dulux Interior Trim semi-gloss
- Ceiling: White on White (brightest)

When guests walk in and say "this is so Hamptons," they're responding to the quantity and quality of white in the room. The white is the point.

## Tip 2: Add a VJ Feature Wall Behind the Sofa

The most transformative single element in our living room: a VJ (V-joint) panelled feature wall painted white behind the sofa. Before it was installed, the room was white and coastal. After: unmistakably Hamptons.

VJ MDF from Bunnings: $30"“$55 per sheet. Standard feature wall (4.2m wide, 2.4m high): approximately 8"“10 sheets = $280"“$550 in materials. A competent DIYer can install it in a weekend. A carpenter will do it for $800"“$1,800 depending on complexity.

Painted in the same white as the walls with a semi-gloss finish (which distinguishes it subtly from the flat-painted wall): the result is architectural detail that no paint treatment achieves.

## Tip 3: Use Navy Only in Cushions, Throws, and One Accent Chair

The Hamptons living room mistake I see most often: too much navy. A navy sofa, navy curtains, navy walls. The result is heavy and dark "” not Hamptons.

Navy belongs in:
- Cushions: two to three navy or navy-striped cushions on the sofa
- A throw: one navy or navy-striped throw over one armchair
- One accent chair: a navy armchair in a corner (optional, one piece only)

That is the entire navy quota for our living room. Everything else is white, natural timber, or jute. The restraint is the point "” the navy reads as a deliberate accent rather than a competing colour.

## Tip 4: Lay the Largest Jute Rug That Fits the Room

The Hamptons living room has a jute, sisal, or natural fibre rug as the floor anchor. Not a patterned rug, not a coloured rug "” a plain, natural-toned rug that connects the room to its coastal origins without competing with the white and navy palette.

**Size rule:** Front legs of sofa and all armchairs on the rug, coffee table fully on the rug. In most Australian living rooms (4Ã—4m+), this means a 240Ã—340cm minimum. We have a 240Ã—340cm jute rug from Temple & Webster ($329) that anchors the full furniture arrangement.

## Tip 5: Choose a White or Pale Timber Coffee Table

The Hamptons coffee table is either white-painted timber (with turned legs "” a classic Hamptons detail) or pale, whitewashed natural timber. Both read as appropriately Hamptons. Dark timber, glass, or industrial metal coffee tables all break the Hamptons palette.

**What I have:** A whitewashed timber coffee table with turned legs from Temple & Webster ($349). It's the most Hamptons piece in the room and one of the most frequently asked about by guests.

## Tip 6: Install or Style White Bookshelves

Built-in white bookshelves are the quintessential Hamptons living room detail "” the backdrop of books, ceramics, and coastal accessories arranged on white painted shelves reads as definitively Hamptons. Built-in shelves require a carpenter ($1,500"“$5,000 depending on size and complexity). Freestanding white IKEA BILLY units ($129"“$299 each), painted the same white as the walls and positioned side by side, create a convincing built-in effect for a fraction of the cost.

**How I style the shelves:**
- Books in the white and cream family, spines facing in (for visual calm)
- Blue and white ceramic pieces grouped in odd numbers
- A few natural elements: small potted plant, a piece of driftwood, a coral
- One framed watercolour seascape

## Tip 7: Hang Linen Curtains at Ceiling Height

The Hamptons living room curtain is a simple, clean linen panel in white or natural "” no pattern, no heavy fabric, no pelmet or rod styling that draws attention. The curtain exists to soften the window and add texture to the wall plane, not to be noticed.

IKEA AINA natural linen curtains ($99 per pair), hung from ceiling-mounted brackets on a simple white curtain rod, achieve the Hamptons effect at a fraction of the cost of custom drapes.

## Tip 8: Add a Lantern or Glass Pendant Light

The Hamptons pendant light is a lantern "” glass and metal, classic proportions, either in black frame (more traditional Hamptons) or brass frame (slightly warmer, very current). Over the coffee table/sofa zone, hung on a cord extension to sitting-height level, a lantern pendant creates the intimate pool of light that makes the Hamptons living room feel like an evening destination.

**What I use:** A trio of clear glass and brushed brass pendant lights hung over the lounge zone, $119 each from Temple & Webster. The brass reads warmer than the traditional black lantern and suits Australian light better.

## Tip 9: Display Blue and White Ceramics as a Collection

The decorative signature of Hamptons style beyond architecture and palette: a collection of blue and white ceramics. Vases in varying heights, a set of blue and white plates on the shelves, or a collection of ceramic objects on the sideboard. Displayed together as a grouping rather than scattered individually, they read as a considered decorative statement.

**Best sources:** Temple & Webster (widest range, $25"“$180 per piece), Kmart (entry-level, $8"“$25), and op shops (often the best vintage pieces for $3"“$15).

## Tip 10: Hang Classic Framed Art "” Seascapes and Botanicals

The Hamptons art palette: framed watercolour seascapes, botanical prints (ferns, palms, or Australian coastal plants in an illustration style), or simple line-drawn coastal maps. All in classic frames "” white painted, natural timber, or occasionally a navy or dark frame for contrast.

**What I have:** Two watercolour seascapes (op shop finds, $12 each in original frames), a fern botanical triptych in white frames from Juniper Print Shop ($115 for three), and a framed antique coastal map from an online map print supplier ($85 including frame). The art is the most personal element of the room.

## Tip 11: Add Nautical Stripe Textiles as the Pattern Signature

The only pattern in a Hamptons living room is the stripe "” navy and white, horizontal or vertical. I use it on two sofa cushions and one outdoor-use throw. No other pattern competes. The stripe is a recognisable Hamptons shorthand, but only works when every other surface is a plain.

**Where to buy:** Temple & Webster has a good range of striped outdoor and indoor cushions ($35"“$80 each). Adairs has striped throws and cushions in the navy and white palette.

## Tip 12: Position a Large Potted Plant in the Corner

A tall indoor plant in a white ceramic or terracotta pot in the corner of the Hamptons living room softens the formality of the white architecture without disrupting the palette. A large fiddle leaf fig (2m+, $80"“$180 from a nursery) or a potted palm adds organic height and connects the interior to the coastal landscape outside.

The white pot is important "” a coloured or heavily textured pot competes with the ceramics display. White unglazed or simple white ceramic lets the plant be the feature.

## Tip 13: Keep the Styling Edited to the Essential

The Hamptons living room is not maximalist. Every surface should have breathing room "” the coffee table styled with three items (a stack of books, a ceramic object, a small plant), the shelves styled with space between groupings, the sofa with seven cushions maximum (three on the sofa, two on each armchair). Objects that don't belong to the palette or the material story are removed, not displaced to a different surface.

The edit is a constant practice, not a one-time event.
