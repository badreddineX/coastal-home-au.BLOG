There's a reason Hamptons style bedrooms never go out of fashion. They're calm, considered, and they feel like the best version of coastal living: relaxed but genuinely refined. According to [Domain.com.au property research](https://www.domain.com.au/research/), homes styled in a Hamptons aesthetic consistently attract premium buyer and renter interest in Australian coastal markets, particularly in Queensland, NSW, and Victoria. I've styled a few bedrooms in this look over the years, and the difference a few key pieces make is remarkable. This guide walks you through every element — bedding, bedheads, colour, flooring, lighting, curtains, artwork, and accessories — with real Australian retailer links and honest AUD prices at every step.

> **Key Takeaways**
> - Linen bedding in white or natural is the single highest-impact change you can make
> - The Hamptons palette is white walls, navy accents, and sandy neutrals — nothing darker
> - You can achieve this look for under ~$800 AUD without touching a single wall
> - [Domain.com.au](https://www.domain.com.au/research/) research shows Hamptons styling lifts perceived property value in Australian coastal markets
> - If you're renting, focus on fully removable changes: bedding, cushions, lamps, and curtains all pack up when you move

## 1. Nail the Colour Palette First

Before you buy a single thing, get the colours right. The Hamptons palette is built on three anchors: crisp white, soft navy, and sandy beige. Everything else in the room should sit within that range. I always start by deciding on the dominant white — warm white suits older homes with timber details, while a cooler bright white suits modern builds with white trim.

Bring in navy as the accent, not the base. A navy bedhead, a navy stripe throw, or two navy cushions are enough. Sandy beige comes from your flooring, a jute rug, or natural linen. **Avoid anything too dark or too saturated:** deep charcoal, forest green, or warm terracotta all pull the room away from the Hamptons feel and into something else entirely.

---

## 2. Choose Linen Bedding in White or Natural

![Layered Hamptons bed with white European pillows, linen quilt, and navy stripe throw](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1522771739844-6a9f6d5f14af?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

Bedding is where the Hamptons bedroom look is won or lost. In Australia's climate, linen is the obvious choice: it regulates temperature through humid Queensland summers and cool Melbourne winters, and it only gets better-looking with every wash. White or natural (undyed) linen is your foundation.

I switched to a 100% linen quilt cover from [The Sheet Society](https://www.thesheetsociety.com/collections/linen) about two years ago, and it's the change I recommend to anyone first. **Expect to spend ~$149-$229 AUD for a quality queen linen quilt cover set.** Layer over the top with a navy stripe or textured cotton throw folded at the foot of the bed, and you've already got 80% of the visual impact sorted.

Stack your pillows in a classic Hamptons arrangement: two European squares at the back, two standard pillows in front, and two cushions at the front in navy or a coastal print. [Adairs](https://www.adairs.com.au/bedroom/) and [Pillow Talk](https://www.pillowtalk.com.au/) both carry Hamptons-appropriate bedding ranges — Adairs tends to be more curated while Pillow Talk offers better value on basics.

---

## 3. Add a Statement Bedhead

The bedhead is the visual centrepiece of a Hamptons bedroom. There are two directions that both work beautifully: an upholstered linen or fabric bedhead in white or soft grey, or a rattan/cane bedhead in natural or white-painted timber. Both read as authentically Hamptons — one leaning more luxe, the other more relaxed coastal.

**A good upholstered queen bedhead from [Temple & Webster](https://www.templeandwebster.com.au/bedroom/) runs ~$300-$800 AUD depending on height and fabric.** Taller bedheads (over 120cm) look more substantial and photograph better for that editorial Hamptons feel. If you're renting and can't wall-mount anything, look for freestanding bedheads that sit behind the mattress — they're widely available and don't require drilling.

## 4. Get the Flooring Right (or Work With What You Have)

![White timber bedframe with rattan bedside table, ceramic lamp, and linen shade in a coastal bedroom](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1556909114-f6e7ad7d3136?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

Authentic Hamptons bedrooms are built on light to mid-tone timber floorboards: natural timber, whitewashed, or pale oak. If you already have light timber floors, you're ahead. If you have carpet or dark floors, a large area rug is the practical solution.

A jute flatweave rug or a cotton loop rug in natural or white placed under the bed (extending at least 60cm on three sides) grounds the whole room in the Hamptons palette. **Jute rugs from [Temple & Webster](https://www.templeandwebster.com.au/rugs/) start from around ~$120-$350 AUD for a queen-sized room.** Avoid anything with pattern or high pile — keep it texture-led and neutral.

One thing most styling guides skip: the rug size matters more than the rug itself. An undersized rug makes even an expensive room feel cheap. Go bigger than you think you need.

---

## 5. Layer Your Lighting

Harsh overhead lighting is the enemy of the Hamptons bedroom. The look relies on soft, layered light from at least two sources: bedside table lamps and either a pendant above the bed or a ceiling fixture on a dimmer. If you can only do one thing, add matching bedside lamps with white linen or fabric shades.

**Ceramic base lamps in white, sandy beige, or coastal blue with a linen shade run ~$79-$200 AUD each at [Temple & Webster](https://www.templeandwebster.com.au/lighting/) and [Freedom](https://www.freedom.com.au/bedroom/).** Buy in matching pairs so both sides of the bed are symmetrical. Symmetry is a core Hamptons principle in the bedroom: it creates calm and visual order. If your budget allows, add a simple white rattan pendant centred over the bed for that signature Hamptons ceiling detail.

## 6. Hang Floor-to-Ceiling Curtains

![Floor-to-ceiling white linen curtains in a Hamptons bedroom with natural light flooding in](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1505693416388-ac5ce068fe85?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

Curtain height transforms a room more than most people expect. The Hamptons rule is simple: hang the rod at ceiling height, not at window height. Even in a standard 2.4m ceiling Australian home, floor-to-ceiling curtains make the room feel taller and more considered.

White or natural linen-look curtains are the signature choice. **[IKEA Australia's](https://www.ikea.com/au/en/rooms/bedroom/) AINA linen curtains (~$99-$149 AUD per pair) are one of the best affordable options for the Hamptons look** — they hang well, come in the right tones, and look far more expensive than the price suggests. For a more premium option, [Adairs](https://www.adairs.com.au/bedroom/) carries wider linen curtain panels that puddle slightly on the floor for a more luxurious effect (~$199-$300 AUD per panel).

If you're renting, a tension rod or a ceiling-mounted curtain track that requires no drilling keeps this change fully reversible.

---

## 7. Style the Bedside Tables

The bedside setup is what separates a styled Hamptons bedroom from a basic one. Both sides should be identical — asymmetry feels restless, not relaxed. Look for bedside tables with a drawer in timber, rattan, or cane detailing in white or natural.

Style each bedside with three elements: a lamp (as above), a small stack of two or three books with the spines turned away, and one organic accent — a coastal candle, a small white ceramic vase with dried grasses, or a textured object in natural stone or bleached timber. **Rattan bedside tables from [Temple & Webster](https://www.templeandwebster.com.au/bedroom/) start from ~$149-$299 AUD each** and are one of the most versatile Hamptons pieces you can own. They work in almost any bedroom configuration.

## 8. Add Artwork With a Coastal Feel

Artwork in a Hamptons bedroom should feel curated but not precious. The easiest approach is a pair of matching prints in simple white frames hung at eye height on either side of the bed, or a single larger artwork centred above the bedhead. Coastal themes work well: abstract coastline sketches, botanical prints in muted tones, or classic nautical maps.

**White-framed prints in A2 or 50x70cm from [Temple & Webster](https://www.templeandwebster.com.au/art/) run ~$49-$149 AUD each.** If you prefer photography, neutral coastal landscapes in black and white feel timeless and don't date. Avoid anything too colourful or graphic — the palette stays restrained in a Hamptons bedroom, and artwork that fights that palette will undermine everything else you've done.

---

## 9. Finish With Accessories and Texture

The final layer is what makes a Hamptons bedroom feel lived-in rather than staged. A natural fibre basket near the wardrobe, a ceramic tray on the dresser, a textured knit throw draped casually over the armchair corner — these small details add warmth without adding clutter.

**A good quality decorative throw from [Adairs](https://www.adairs.com.au/bedroom/) runs ~$79-$149 AUD** and should be chunky-knit, waffle weave, or a fine stripe in white, navy, or oatmeal. Dried coastal grasses in a simple white vase (tall, thin-necked) are the current Hamptons styling staple — they're low maintenance, affordable, and photograph beautifully.

I keep a shallow rattan tray on the dresser with a candle, a small plant, and a perfume bottle. It takes less than two minutes to set up and makes the whole dresser look intentional rather than cluttered.

---

## 10. Pull It Together With a Styling Run-Through

Once everything is in place, do a final styling walk-through before you stop. Stand at the bedroom door and look at the room as a whole. The Hamptons bedroom should read as calm, light, and symmetrical from that doorway view. If something pulls your eye and feels out of place, it probably is.

Common fixes at this stage: too many cushions (edit down to five maximum), a lamp that's too short for the bedside table (the shade base should sit near eye height when you're lying down), or curtains that aren't hanging straight. **Getting this right is worth ten minutes of adjusting** — it's the difference between a room that feels styled and one that just has nice individual pieces.

If you're renting, here's the key point: focus on fully removable changes. Bedding, cushions, lamps, and curtains all pack up when you move. The entire look I've described above can be taken with you — none of it requires you to touch a wall, floor, or ceiling permanently.
