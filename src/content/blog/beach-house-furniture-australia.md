There's a furniture graveyard hidden behind most Australian beach houses. Rusted iron chairs. Fabric sofas with salt-stained cushions. Timber side tables that warped within a season. The coastal environment is genuinely punishing on furniture that wasn't chosen with it in mind.

[Domain.com.au's coastal property data](https://www.domain.com.au/research/) consistently shows that coastal homes within 5km of the waterfront trade at a significant premium — and interior presentation drives a meaningful portion of that value. [Choice.com.au](https://www.choice.com.au/home-improvement/decorating-and-furnishing/furniture/articles/sustainable-furniture-buying-guide) notes that Australians are increasingly prioritising natural, durable materials in home furnishings, a trend that aligns perfectly with what the coastal environment demands anyway.

I've furnished coastal homes on the Gold Coast and Sunshine Coast over six years. The lessons were expensive. This is the version of the furniture guide I wish I'd had at the start.

## 1. The Linen Sofa: The Non-Negotiable Foundation Piece

The sofa is the most important furniture decision in any living space — and in an Australian beach house, the material choice narrows significantly. A dark leather sofa reads urban. A velvet sofa reads formal. A white or natural linen sofa reads exactly right.

Linen upholstery breathes in coastal heat, softens beautifully with use, and holds the warm, relaxed quality that defines the Australian beach house interior. It's also the material that photographs most beautifully in the natural light that floods a coastal home.

**What to look for in a coastal linen sofa:**
- 100% linen or high-percentage linen blend upholstery (not "linen look" polyester, which doesn't breathe)
- Removable, washable covers — sand and salt air will require regular washing
- A solid timber or steel frame (avoid particleboard frames in humid environments — they deteriorate)
- Low-profile or mid-century silhouette in white, natural, or warm oat tones

**Where to buy:** Freedom's linen sofa range ($1,800-$4,500 AUD) is consistently the best quality-to-aesthetic ratio in Australia. Temple & Webster carries a wide range from ~$900-$2,500 AUD. IKEA AU's UPPLAND linen sofa ($799-$999 AUD) is the best budget coastal sofa available — the covers are washable and the aesthetic is genuinely right.

**Key point:** A natural linen sofa from Temple & Webster or Freedom (~$900-$4,500 AUD) is the single furniture piece that most defines the Australian beach house interior — invest in quality here before anywhere else.

## 2. The Rattan Armchair: Texture, Warmth, and Coastal Identity

Every Australian beach house living room needs at least one [rattan](/blog/rattan-furniture-australia) armchair. It's the piece that immediately signals "coastal home" without trying — and it provides the warm texture that balances the lightness of white walls and linen.

![Rattan armchair with white linen cushion beside a tall indoor plant in a bright coastal Australian living room](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1555041469-a586c61ea9bc?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

Natural rattan is the authentic choice for indoor use — it ages beautifully, developing a warm honey-to-caramel patina over years. Use synthetic rattan only for outdoor applications.

**The rule:** One or two significant rattan chairs per living room. More than that tips the aesthetic from coastal to tropical resort.

**Where to buy:**
- Kmart: entry-level rattan occasional chairs from ~$89-$149 AUD (good starting point, won't last 10 years)
- Temple & Webster: mid-range rattan armchairs from ~$250-$650 AUD (solid quality, wide range)
- Freedom: higher-end rattan and cane armchairs from ~$600-$1,500 AUD (investment quality)
- Globewest: designer-level rattan furniture from ~$800-$2,500 AUD

*If renting, rattan armchairs are light and completely freestanding — the ideal furniture addition that moves with you.*

**Key point:** A rattan armchair from Temple & Webster (~$250-$650 AUD) is the most cost-effective single piece for establishing a coastal aesthetic in an Australian beach house living room.

## 3. The Coastal Dining Table: Pale Timber or Teak for the Win

The [dining room](/blog/coastal-dining-room-australia) table is the social centre of a beach house. It handles more practical punishment than any other piece of furniture — sand tracked in on feet, salt spray through open doors, spilled wine during long lunches, and direct coastal humidity every day.

The wrong material choice here is obvious and expensive. The right choice is almost invisible — because good coastal dining furniture just looks right and keeps looking right for years.

**The best materials for a coastal Australian dining table:**

**Teak:** The premium choice. Naturally water-resistant, handles humidity and salt air, and develops a beautiful silver-grey patina outdoors or a warm honey colour when maintained indoors with teak oil. Temple & Webster carries plantation teak dining tables from ~$700-$2,000 AUD. Outdoor Elegance has higher-end options.

**Pale reclaimed or solid Australian hardwood:** Plantation timber in a light stain or natural finish is the most authentically Australian choice. Solid timber construction (not veneer over particleboard) is essential in coastal humidity. Local furniture makers are the best source.

**Powder-coated aluminium with timber top:** The modern coastal option. Lightweight, completely rust-proof legs with a warm timber top. Excellent for indoor-outdoor dining rooms that open to an alfresco. Temple & Webster and Bunnings both carry these from ~$500-$1,200 AUD.

**What to avoid:** Dark-stained timber (absorbs light and reads too formal), high-gloss lacquered surfaces (show every watermark), and glass tops (impractical in a beach house with children or frequent guests).

**Key point:** A pale solid-timber or plantation teak dining table from Temple & Webster (~$700-$2,000 AUD) suits Australian coastal dining rooms and handles the humidity and salt air that coastal living involves.

## 4. Dining Chairs: Rattan, Linen, or Both Together

Dining chairs are one of the most satisfying places to introduce rattan into a beach house interior. A rattan or cane dining chair at a pale timber table is the visual combination that defines the coastal Australian dining room, and it's available at every price point.



**Options at different budgets:**

**Budget ($60-$130 per chair):** Kmart and IKEA AU both have rattan-look or natural rattan dining chairs in this range. The IKEA AU VOXLÖV rattan dining chair ($129 each) is genuinely beautiful and holds up well. Kmart's natural rattan chair ($69) is a good starter.

**Mid-range ($130-$350 per chair):** Temple & Webster stocks a wide range of rattan, cane, and coastal timber dining chairs at this price point — the widest selection available online in Australia.

**Investment ($350-$700+ per chair):** Globewest, Coco Republic, and local furniture makers at this level. These chairs are built for decades of use.

**The linen dining chair alternative:** If rattan isn't right for your table style, a simple linen-upholstered dining chair in white or warm oat achieves a similarly coastal result. Look for removable covers — beach house dining chairs get sandy.

**Key point:** Rattan dining chairs from Temple & Webster or IKEA AU ($69-$350 AUD each) create the definitive coastal Australian dining room combination when paired with a pale timber table.

## 5. The Outdoor Dining Setting: Teak or Powder-Coated Aluminium Only

The outdoor dining setting is the piece Australian beach house owners under-invest in most consistently — and then replace most frequently as a result. A $400 pressed-steel-and-polyester set from a discount warehouse won't survive two Queensland summers.

![Teak outdoor dining table set for six on a covered alfresco deck with coastal garden visible beyond](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1600566753086-00f18fb6b3ea?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

**The only materials worth buying for Australian coastal outdoor dining:**

**Teak:** The best long-term investment. Left untreated, it weathers to a beautiful silver-grey; oiled annually, it maintains a warm honey colour. A quality six-seater teak setting from Temple & Webster runs ~$1,200-$3,500 AUD; from Outdoor Elegance or Gloster, expect $3,000-$8,000 AUD for a set that will last 20+ years.

**Powder-coated aluminium:** The best value-to-durability option. Completely rust-proof, lightweight enough to move easily, and available in a wide range of styles from minimal to Hamptons. A six-seater aluminium setting from Temple & Webster runs ~$800-$2,000 AUD.

**Plantation eucalyptus or acacia:** A slightly lower price point than teak with very similar performance. Temple & Webster stocks several eucalyptus settings from ~$600-$1,200 AUD that are excellent value.

*If renting with a small balcony, a compact two-seater bistro set in aluminium from Bunnings ($150-$300 AUD) is the practical choice — foldable, lightweight, and leaves with you.*

**Key point:** A powder-coated aluminium six-seater outdoor dining setting from Temple & Webster (~$800-$2,000 AUD) is the best value coastal outdoor dining investment for most Australian beach houses.

## 6. The Coastal Bed Frame: Rattan or Cane Bedhead

The bed frame is the single piece of furniture that most defines the aesthetic of a coastal bedroom — and the rattan or cane bedhead is its most recognisable expression in Australian coastal interiors.

A rattan or woven cane bedhead in a queen or king size introduces texture, warmth, and organic shape to the bedroom. Paired with warm white walls and linen bedding, it creates the defining Australian beach house bedroom image without requiring any other significant changes.

**What to look for:**
- Natural rattan or woven cane construction (not moulded synthetic, which looks cheap close-up)
- A headboard-only configuration rather than a full bed frame, which gives flexibility with base selection
- A height of at least 120cm for a queen bed for visual impact — shorter headboards disappear in a room

**Where to buy:**
- Temple & Webster: natural rattan and cane bedheads from ~$299-$799 AUD (queen to king)
- Freedom: higher-end cane and rattan bed frames from ~$700-$2,000 AUD
- Adairs: seasonal rattan bedheads at mid-range prices, worth checking for sale periods
- Kmart: entry-level rattan bedheads from ~$149-$249 AUD (good aesthetically, lighter construction)

**Key point:** A natural rattan or cane bedhead from Temple & Webster (~$299-$799 AUD) is the highest-impact single change in an Australian coastal bedroom.

## 7. Bedside Tables: Timber, Rattan, or Ceramic for the Coastal Bedroom

The bedside table is a place where coastal beach house styling often goes wrong — either too dark (heavy dark timber nightstands from a mainstream furniture store), too minimal (a floating shelf that holds a phone and nothing else), or too trendy (mirrored or high-gloss surfaces that fight the linen and rattan).

**The coastal beach house bedside table:**
- Natural pale timber: Simple and right. Temple & Webster stocks solid oak and light timber bedside tables from ~$150-$450 AUD.
- Rattan or cane: A small rattan side table used as a bedside adds another layer of coastal texture. Temple & Webster and Kmart both carry these.
- Ceramic garden stool: Used as a bedside table, a ceramic garden stool in white or a simple pattern is surprisingly beautiful and practical. Kmart stocks them from ~$39-$69 AUD.
- Woven or wicker: Small wicker bedside tables in natural tones are a classic coastal choice available from Temple & Webster from ~$180-$350 AUD.

**What to avoid:** Dark-stained timber, high-gloss lacquer, and mirrored surfaces — all of which work against the warm, natural aesthetic of a coastal bedroom.

**Key point:** A natural timber or rattan bedside table from Temple & Webster or Kmart (~$39-$450 AUD) completes the coastal bedroom furniture arrangement without competing with the bedhead or bedding.

## 8. The Outdoor Lounge Setting: Synthetic Rattan Over Aluminium



Every Australian beach house — whether a permanent home or a holiday property — needs an outdoor lounge zone. It's used as frequently as the indoor living room for much of the year, and in Queensland and WA, arguably more so.

The correct material for outdoor lounge furniture in a coastal environment is synthetic rattan (also called all-weather wicker or resin wicker) over an aluminium frame. This combination:
- Looks like natural rattan but handles UV without cracking or fading
- Has aluminium frames that won't rust in salt air
- Uses quick-dry foam cushions that resist mould in coastal humidity

**A functional outdoor lounge setting needs:**
- A two or three-seat outdoor sofa
- Two armchairs or one sofa plus a chaise/daybed for larger spaces
- A weatherproof coffee table (aluminium or teak)
- Two side tables minimum

**Where to buy:**
- Temple & Webster: full synthetic rattan outdoor lounge settings from ~$800-$2,500 AUD
- Bunnings: entry-level outdoor lounge sets from ~$400-$900 AUD (good for a rented property or starter home)
- Outdoor Elegance: premium outdoor settings from ~$2,000-$8,000 AUD for investment-quality pieces
- IKEA AU: the NÄMMARÖ and JUTHOLMEN outdoor ranges offer reasonable quality at $300-$700 AUD for a basic setting

**Key point:** A synthetic rattan outdoor lounge setting over aluminium frames from Temple & Webster (~$800-$2,500 AUD) is the correct coastal outdoor furniture investment for any Australian beach house.

## 9. Storage Furniture: Rattan Baskets and Timber Consoles

![Natural rattan baskets on open timber shelving in a coastal Australian entryway, styled with a ceramic vase and coastal art](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1618220179428-22790b461013?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

Beach houses generate more practical storage challenges than most other homes. Sand comes in from the beach. Outdoor toys, towels, wetsuits, and beach gear need somewhere to live. The challenge is solving the storage problem without introducing furniture that looks like it belongs in a city apartment.

**Coastal-appropriate storage furniture:**

**Large rattan or wicker baskets:** The multi-purpose coastal storage solution. They hold blankets, toys, beach gear, and anything else that needs corralling. Kmart stocks quality large rattan baskets from ~$25-$79 AUD. Temple & Webster has a wider range of sizes from ~$45-$200 AUD.

**Timber console with open shelving:** A narrow timber console in the entryway or hallway is the right piece for a beach house entry. Open lower shelves hold baskets for shoes and beach gear. Look for solid pale timber or a white-painted finish — not veneer, which delaminates in coastal humidity.

**Timber hall bench with storage:** An entryway bench with baskets or a storage ottoman below is the practical coastal entry piece. Bunnings and Temple & Webster carry these from ~$150-$500 AUD.

**Key point:** A set of three large rattan baskets from Kmart (~$25-$79 AUD each) solves the beach house storage challenge while adding coastal texture — one of the highest value-to-impact buys in this guide.

## 10. Coffee Tables and Side Tables: Low, Light, and Natural

The indoor coffee table in an Australian beach house should feel light, natural, and easy to move. Heavy marble-topped tables and dark carved-timber coffee tables don't belong in a relaxed coastal home — they import the wrong energy.

**The best coastal coffee table configurations:**
- **Pale timber top with hairpin or tube legs:** Light, airy, and effortlessly coastal. Temple & Webster and IKEA AU carry solid oak options from ~$200-$600 AUD.
- **Rattan coffee table with storage shelf below:** Adds texture and practical storage. Temple & Webster from ~$250-$550 AUD.
- **Two ottomans in place of a table:** A deeply relaxed beach house choice — two large linen or woven ottomans can be used as seating, surface, or footrest depending on the occasion. Freedom and Temple & Webster carry quality options from ~$200-$600 AUD each.
- **A stack of large-format art books:** Not a table, but a surface. A stack of three or four books topped with a ceramic tray is a genuine design hack for small coastal living rooms.

**Key point:** A pale timber coffee table from Temple & Webster or IKEA AU (~$200-$600 AUD) keeps the coastal living room light and open rather than grounded and heavy.

## 11. Outdoor Sun Loungers: Where the Beach House Fantasy Lives

The outdoor sun lounger is the piece of furniture that transforms a beach house into a resort — and it's the piece most Australians either overlook or buy too cheaply. A sun lounger that sags after one season, or tips on an uneven deck, is more frustrating than not having one at all.

**What to look for:**
- An aluminium or teak frame (not steel, which rusts; not plastic, which becomes brittle in UV)
- Quick-dry cushion — essential in a coastal environment where the lounger gets wet from sea spray, rain, or a dripping swimmer
- Adjustable backrest with multiple positions
- Non-marking feet for decks and tiles

**Where to buy:**
- Temple & Webster: aluminium sun loungers from ~$250-$700 AUD per unit
- Outdoor Elegance: premium teak and aluminium loungers from ~$600-$2,000 AUD each
- Bunnings: entry-level aluminium loungers from ~$150-$350 AUD (good for a holiday rental, less suited to permanent use)
- IKEA AU: the VÄSMÖ outdoor sun lounger ($199 AUD) is a surprisingly strong option at its price point

*If renting a beach house, a pair of quality sun loungers are freestanding and move with you — one of the best lifestyle investments for a coastal renter.*

**Key point:** A pair of aluminium sun loungers from Temple & Webster (~$250-$700 AUD each) completes the Australian beach house outdoor experience and are the pieces guests always gravitate to first.

---

Good beach house furniture isn't about buying everything at once — it's about buying the right things once. Start with the linen sofa and the outdoor dining setting: those two pieces, chosen well, establish the aesthetic and last years without attention. Add the rattan pieces over time. Don't rush the layering. The beach houses that feel most genuinely coastal are the ones that accumulated slowly and edited often.
