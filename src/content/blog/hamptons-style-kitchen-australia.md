The Hamptons kitchen is the most coveted kitchen style in Australian homes right now — and for good reason. It's the balance of elegant and functional that suits the way Australians actually cook and entertain. According to [Domain.com.au property research](https://www.domain.com.au/research/), Hamptons-style kitchens consistently rank among the top design features buyers nominate when searching for premium coastal properties. I've pulled together this guide based on years of following Australian kitchen renovations, and what I've found is that even a partial Hamptons update — new handles, a subway tile splashback, styled open shelves — transforms the feel of a kitchen dramatically. This post covers every element from cabinetry to lighting, with AUD prices and real Australian retailers throughout.



> **Key Takeaways**
> - Shaker cabinet doors are the defining Hamptons element — they're available as a DIY upgrade without replacing full cabinetry
> - White subway tiles remain the most cost-effective splashback choice, delivering outsized visual impact
> - Brushed brass hardware (handles, tapware, pendants) is the detail that signals quality throughout the space
> - Open shelving styled with white ceramics and timber boards adds the lived-in coastal warmth that closed cabinets can't
> - A Hamptons kitchen refresh (handles + splashback + benchtop styling) is achievable for under $3,000 AUD without renovation

## 1. Start With Shaker Cabinet Doors — The Defining Element

No single element defines a Hamptons kitchen more than shaker-profile cabinet doors. The recessed panel, the clean lines, the way they read as both traditional and contemporary — this is the DNA of the style. If your kitchen currently has flat-panel or heavily ornate doors, switching to shaker profile is the highest-impact upgrade you can make.

The good news is you don't need to rip out your entire cabinetry to do it. Shaker doors can be retrofitted to existing cabinet boxes in most cases. Kaboodle Kitchen at Bunnings offers a full range of shaker-profile doors and drawer fronts from ~$180–$350 AUD per door depending on size. For a higher-end result, a local cabinet maker can supply custom shaker doors in your preferred colour and profile from ~$250–$500 AUD per door.

**Key point:** Paint your shaker cabinet doors in Dulux Natural White or Vivid White for the classic Hamptons look. In Australian kitchens, warm white consistently outperforms cool white under our intense natural light — it glows rather than glares.

Renters: cabinet doors are off-limits, but new hardware (see tip 5) and a styled benchtop can shift the look meaningfully without touching the structure.

In my own kitchen, I replaced the flat-panel doors on our Gold Coast rental's cabinetry when we moved into our own home with Kaboodle shaker-profile doors from Bunnings (~$210 AUD per door for the 600mm wide version). The difference was immediate and significant — the kitchen went from looking like a 2008 spec build to something I'd genuinely choose. Total spend for 14 doors was around $2,940 AUD, and I did the swap over a weekend without a tradesperson.

## 2. Is a Two-Tone Cabinet Palette Worth It?

The two-tone kitchen — white upper cabinets and navy (or dark green) lower cabinets — has become one of the most recognisable Hamptons kitchen variations in Australian homes. It adds depth and visual interest that an all-white kitchen can sometimes lack, and it grounds the space beautifully.

 The key that most Australian kitchen renovators miss is the importance of proportion in a two-tone scheme. The navy or coloured base should extend no higher than benchtop level. When coloured cabinets extend into the upper cabinetry zone, the kitchen reads as overwhelming rather than considered. Keep the dark tone low, the light tone high, and the transition at the benchtop line.

**Key point:** A navy lower cabinet can be achieved with a repaint in Dulux Deep Ocean or a similar deep navy, using Dulux Aquanamel enamel (~$65–$95 AUD per 1L) — a resilient, washable finish suited to kitchen cabinetry. A professional kitchen repaint costs ~$1,500–$3,500 AUD depending on kitchen size and number of doors.

## 3. What Splashback Tile Suits a Hamptons Kitchen?

White subway tile is the default Hamptons splashback — and it earns its reputation. The horizontal running bond layout creates a timeless, classic surface that works with both traditional and contemporary kitchens. It's also one of the most affordable ways to upgrade a kitchen surface with genuine impact.

The standard subway tile is 75x150mm in a glossy white ceramic finish. For a more contemporary Hamptons adaptation, a larger format tile — 100x300mm or 100x200mm — has a cleaner, more streamlined look that suits Australian kitchens well. A bevelled edge adds subtle shadow detail and depth. Grout colour matters enormously: bright white grout reads clean and contemporary, while a light grey grout adds warmth and hides everyday kitchen grime far better.

**Key point:** Standard white subway tiles cost ~$25–$60 AUD per m2 from Bunnings or tile specialists like Beaumont Tiles. A typical kitchen splashback of 3–5m2 costs ~$300–$600 AUD in tiles alone, plus installation. It's one of the best value-for-impact upgrades in this entire guide.

When I renovated our kitchen splashback, I chose a bevelled 75x150mm white subway tile from Beaumont Tiles (~$38 AUD per m2) with a pale grey grout from Bunnings (~$18 AUD per bag). The tiler charged ~$480 AUD for a 4.5m2 splashback including labour, and the result was genuinely transformative — the kitchen read as properly designed rather than builder-grade within three days of work.

## 4. What Benchtop Material Works Best for a Hamptons Kitchen in Australia?

The benchtop is the workhorse of the kitchen and the element that most communicates quality at a glance. In a Hamptons kitchen, the benchtop should be light, clean, and visually substantial — thick enough to look luxurious, pale enough to reflect the coastal light palette.

Natural marble is the original Hamptons benchtop material but it's porous, acid-sensitive, and genuinely difficult to maintain in an active kitchen. In Australian homes, engineered stone — Caesarstone, Smartstone, or Silestone — delivers the same visual result with dramatically better durability. White or light grey finishes with subtle veining (Caesarstone Statuario Nuvo or Calacatta Nuvo) are the most popular Hamptons choices.

For a budget alternative, a white laminate benchtop with a stone-look finish costs ~$150–$350 AUD per linear metre and reads reasonably well in photographs, though it doesn't have the visual weight or longevity of stone. Timber benchtops in a bleached or natural finish suit a warmer, more casual Hamptons interpretation beautifully.

**Key point:** Engineered stone benchtops cost ~$600–$1,200 AUD per linear metre installed through kitchen specialists. Timber benchtops start from ~$300–$600 AUD per linear metre. IKEA AU offers laminate benchtop options from ~$150 AUD per linear metre as a budget entry point.

In my own kitchen, I specified Caesarstone Calacatta Nuvo in a 20mm thickness for the island bench (~$820 AUD per linear metre installed, 2.8 linear metres total). It's now two years in and the surface still looks immaculate despite daily use and the occasional red wine incident. The engineered stone held up far better than I expected, and I've had neighbours ask if it's real marble every time they visit.

## 5. Why Do Hardware Handles Matter So Much?

Hardware is the jewellery of a kitchen. The wrong handles make even beautiful cabinetry look unfinished. The right handles — chosen deliberately to suit the style — tie the whole space together in a way that's disproportionate to their cost.

For a Hamptons kitchen, the clear choice is brushed brass or antique brass. It's warm, it's elegant, and it connects directly to the coastal New England heritage of the style. Matte black is a contemporary alternative that works well in a two-tone kitchen. Avoid polished chrome in a Hamptons kitchen — it reads as cold and commercial against the warmth of white and timber that the style depends on.

Bar handles (long, horizontal) suit a Hamptons kitchen better than round knobs for a contemporary feel. Shaker-profile doors with long bar handles in brushed brass is one of the most recognisable Australian coastal kitchen combinations right now.

**Key point:** Quality brushed brass bar handles cost ~$8–$25 AUD each from Bunnings hardware section. A full kitchen of 20 handles and knobs costs ~$160–$500 AUD — and the replacement takes an afternoon. This is the single most cost-effective Hamptons kitchen upgrade available.

When I renovated, I replaced 22 flat chrome handles with brushed brass 160mm bar handles from Bunnings (~$12 AUD each, total $264 AUD). The swap took a Saturday afternoon with just a screwdriver. The kitchen looked like a different room when I finished — the warmth the brass brought to the white cabinetry was genuinely surprising for a change that cost under $300 AUD.

## 6. Should You Add Open Shelving in a Hamptons Kitchen?

Open shelving is the element that makes a Hamptons kitchen feel lived-in and styled rather than showroom-perfect. A run of floating timber shelves above the benchtop — or flanking the rangehood — gives the kitchen personality and an opportunity to display the ceramics and timber pieces that make the coastal aesthetic complete.

The common mistake is over-filling open shelves. The Hamptons approach is restrained: a few carefully chosen pieces with deliberate spacing between them. White ceramic canisters, a timber chopping board stood upright, a small coastal plant, some simple stacked plates in white. These are the items that photograph beautifully and age well.

**Key point:** Floating timber shelves in raw oak or a whitewashed finish cost ~$80–$200 AUD per shelf from Bunnings or a local timber supplier. The brackets should be substantial enough to carry weight — avoid flimsy concealed brackets for shelves that will hold ceramics. For renters, a freestanding shelving unit works just as well and requires no wall fixing.

## 7. How Should You Choose a Hamptons Kitchen Tapware?

Tapware is a detail that most people choose last and regret rushing. In a Hamptons kitchen, the tap is visible from almost every angle in the space — it should be chosen as carefully as the cabinetry handles.

Brushed brass or aged brass is the first choice for a true Hamptons look. It connects to the heritage of the style and pairs beautifully with both white and two-tone cabinet palettes. Brushed nickel is the more neutral alternative — it sits between chrome and gold, suits a wider range of kitchen colours, and is easier to keep clean than polished finishes. Matte black works in a contemporary Hamptons kitchen but can feel too dramatic if the rest of the space is pale and light-filled.

The most Hamptons-appropriate tap profile is a high-arc or gooseneck form — the tall, arched spout that lets you fill large pots without difficulty and reads visually as confident and deliberate.

**Key point:** A quality brushed brass kitchen mixer tap costs ~$180–$500 AUD from Bunnings or specialist tapware retailers. Cheaper alternatives from IKEA AU or Bunnings' own range start from ~$80–$150 AUD. Hire a licensed plumber for installation — it's not worth DIY-ing tapware for the sake of $150 in labour.

## 8. What Pendant Lights Work in a Hamptons Kitchen?

Pendant lights over the island bench or dining table are among the most photographed elements of any Hamptons kitchen. They're structural enough to be a design statement, but replaceable enough to use as a pivot point when refreshing the style on a budget.

The classic Hamptons pendant is a rattan or woven shade, or a glass dome with brass fittings. Rattan pendants add warmth and coastal texture — they're the most distinctly Australian take on the Hamptons kitchen light. A glass schoolhouse pendant with a brass fitting is closer to the American original and looks stunning over a marble or stone island.

Hang them low enough to be impactful — the bottom of the shade should sit approximately 750–850mm above the benchtop surface. Pair them in a set of two or three over a long island for a balanced, considered look.

**Key point:** Quality rattan or woven pendant lights cost ~$80–$250 AUD each from Temple & Webster or Freedom. Glass schoolhouse pendants with brass fittings start from ~$120–$350 AUD each. IKEA AU carries budget-friendly rattan-style pendants from ~$39–$69 AUD that read well in the space.

## 9. What Colour Palette Best Defines the Hamptons Kitchen?

The Hamptons kitchen palette is disciplined — and that restraint is the source of its elegance. The base is always white or warm off-white. The accent is navy, soft grey, or muted sage. The warmth comes from natural timber, brass, and woven textures.

Australian homes benefit from adapting the palette slightly toward the warm side. Pure cool white walls and cabinets look beautiful in the overcast New England light of the original Hamptons aesthetic, but in Brisbane or Perth they can read as clinical and harsh by midday. A warm white — Dulux Natural White, Lexicon Quarter, or Antique White USA — is a far more flattering base in Australian light conditions.

 I've found that the most successful Australian Hamptons kitchens use a three-colour discipline: white as the dominant (70%), one accent colour such as navy or sage (20%), and warm natural tones in timber and brass (10%). When clients deviate from this ratio — adding terracotta, deep green, and navy all at once — the kitchen stops reading as Hamptons and starts reading as confused.

**Key point:** Sample your chosen whites on the actual wall in your kitchen before committing. The light changes dramatically throughout the day in Australian homes, and a white that looks perfect in the morning can appear yellow by afternoon. Dulux and Taubmans both offer sample pots from ~$8 AUD.

## 10. How Do You Style a Hamptons Kitchen Benchtop?

A styled benchtop is the difference between a kitchen that looks renovated and one that looks designed. The Hamptons approach to benchtop styling is minimal and intentional — a few well-chosen objects rather than a cluttered collection of appliances.

The classic Hamptons benchtop vignette: a timber chopping board stood upright against the splashback, a small pot of fresh herbs, a white ceramic canister set for flour and sugar, and a simple linen tea towel folded neatly over the oven handle. That's it. The rule is: if it doesn't earn its place visually, store it inside.

Conceal everyday appliances — the toaster, the coffee machine, the blender — in an appliance garage or inside the cabinetry where possible. A clear benchtop reads as more generous and expensive regardless of what the cabinetry beneath it looks like.

**Key point:** A set of three white ceramic canisters costs ~$40–$120 AUD from Temple & Webster or Adairs. A good quality timber chopping board costs ~$30–$80 AUD. These two items alone — placed on a cleared benchtop — shift the entire feel of the kitchen toward Hamptons instantly.

## 11. What Flooring Suits a Hamptons Kitchen?

Flooring connects the kitchen to the rest of the home and should be chosen with the full house in mind. The most authentic Hamptons kitchen floors are wide-plank timber in a bleached, natural, or light grey-washed finish. They're warm, they photograph beautifully, and they connect the kitchen to the coastal aesthetic that runs through the whole home.

In Australia, engineered timber flooring is more practical than solid timber in kitchens because it handles the humidity fluctuations and occasional water exposure that kitchens inevitably see. Wide-plank engineered oak in a natural or whitewashed finish is the closest Australian equivalent to the classic Hamptons floor.

For tile lovers, a large-format stone-look porcelain tile in white or pale grey reads beautifully in a Hamptons kitchen — it's also the most practical option for households with young children and pets, given its near-indestructibility.

**Key point:** Engineered timber flooring for a kitchen costs ~$60–$150 AUD per m2 for materials, plus installation. Porcelain stone-look tiles range from ~$35–$90 AUD per m2. Both are available through Bunnings and specialist flooring retailers across Australia.

## Start Here: Priority Order

Whether you're planning a full renovation or a cosmetic refresh, follow this sequence to make the most of your budget.

| Priority | Action | Approx. Cost |
|----------|--------|--------------|
| 1 | Replace cabinet handles with brushed brass bar handles | ~$160–$500 AUD |
| 2 | Add a white subway tile splashback (or regrout and reclean existing tiles) | ~$300–$1,500 AUD |
| 3 | Replace or repaint cabinet doors to a shaker profile in warm white or navy base | ~$2,000–$8,000 AUD |
| 4 | Upgrade benchtop to engineered stone or timber | ~$3,000–$8,000 AUD installed |
| 5 | Add a rattan or glass pendant light over the island | ~$150–$500 AUD |

The Hamptons kitchen is achievable at almost every budget level. A set of brass handles and a styled benchtop costs under $500 AUD. A full shaker cabinet renovation with stone benchtop and subway tile splashback lands at $15,000–$30,000 AUD. Start where your budget allows and add layers over time.
