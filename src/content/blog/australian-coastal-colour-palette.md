Colour is the cheapest and most transformative thing you can do to a coastal home. The wrong palette makes a room feel cold, heavy, or like a holiday rental that's trying too hard. The right one makes every surface feel like it was designed for that specific quality of coastal Australian light.

Getting coastal colour right in Australia is different from getting it right in, say, coastal France or the Hamptons. [Dulux Australia's colour trend research](https://www.dulux.com.au/colour/colour-forecast) consistently shows that Australian homeowners favour warmer, sun-bleached tones over the cooler, more saturated coastal palettes popular in Northern Hemisphere contexts. Our light is stronger, warmer, and more direct — and our colour choices need to account for that.

I've repainted coastal rooms more times than I care to admit. These are the nine combinations I come back to reliably, along with the specific paint names and codes that make them work.

![Bright coastal Australian living room with warm white walls, natural timber floors, and soft ocean-blue cushion accents](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1600210492486-724fe5c67fb0?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

> **Key Takeaways**
> - Warm whites (not cool whites) are the correct wall foundation for every Australian coastal room
> - Sandy neutrals bridge white walls and warm timber without reading as dated beige
> - Ocean blues and greens belong in accents and accessories, not on primary wall surfaces
> - Dulux and Taubmans both make paint specifically rated for coastal exterior conditions
> - According to Dulux Australia's colour research, warm sun-bleached tones consistently outperform cooler coastal palettes in Australian light conditions



## 1. The Classic: Warm White Plus Sandy Neutral Plus Natural Timber

This is the palette every other Australian coastal combination builds from. It's not boring — it's foundational. A warm white wall, sandy neutral trim and joinery, and natural timber floors or furniture is the colour relationship that the Australian coastal interior depends on.

**Why it works:** The three tones share the same warm undertone. Nothing clashes. Everything connects to the landscape outside — the bleached sand, the warm timber of a beach house deck, the white of sea foam. Natural light flatters all three colours equally.

**The specific paints:**

- **Walls:** Dulux Natural White (half-strength) — this is the single most-used coastal wall colour in Australia for good reason. It's warm without being yellow, neutral without being cold. Available from Bunnings in 4L ($65-$75 AUD) and 10L ($135-$160 AUD).
- **Trim and joinery:** Dulux Antique White USA full-strength — slightly warmer than Natural White, creates a gentle tonal shift between wall and trim that reads sophisticated rather than stark. Same price range.
- **Accent furniture and textiles:** Sandy beige, raw linen, jute. All the furniture and textile colours of the classic Australian beach house slot directly into this palette.

 In our Gold Coast home, this exact palette — Natural White half-strength on walls, Antique White USA on the skirting boards and window frames — made a north-facing open-plan living room feel like a different house within a weekend. The warm undertones picked up the afternoon light and amplified it throughout the space in a way that cool white never achieved.

**Key point:** Dulux Natural White (half-strength) on walls, available from Bunnings at ~$65-$75 AUD for 4L, is the safest and most beautiful starting point for any Australian coastal interior.

## 2. Sun-Bleached: Warm White Plus Linen Wash Plus Driftwood Grey

This palette is for the Australian beach house that feels genuinely worn-in, lived-in, and connected to the landscape rather than freshly styled. It's warmer and more textural than the classic white palette — the right choice for older homes, character properties, and anywhere you want depth over brightness.

![A sun-bleached coastal bedroom with soft driftwood grey bedhead, warm linen bedding, and sheer white curtains](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1560448204-603b3fc33ddc?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

**The specific paints:**

- **Walls:** Taubmans Flaxen (a warm, barely-there sand tone) — noticeably warmer than white, reads as a neutral rather than a colour. Works particularly well in rooms that don't receive strong direct light. Taubmans 4L interior from Bunnings, ~$60-$70 AUD.
- **Feature or accent wall:** Dulux Linen Wash — a soft, warm off-white with visible warmth. Beautiful in a bedroom or living room where you want a tonal feature without committing to a colour.
- **Trim:** Dulux Hog Bristle (a light warm grey-beige) — connects the wall tone to natural timber without going too dark.

**Where this palette belongs:**
- Master bedroom: Flaxen walls, white linen bedding, driftwood-grey rattan bedhead
- Living room: Linen Wash feature wall behind the sofa, Natural White on remaining walls
- Entryway and hallway: Flaxen throughout — warm and welcoming without the starkness of pure white

**Key point:** Taubmans Flaxen on walls with Dulux Hog Bristle trim (~$60-$75 AUD per 4L at Bunnings) creates the warm, sun-bleached Australian coastal interior that feels genuinely lived-in rather than newly decorated.

## 3. Ocean Calm: Warm White Plus Soft Sage Green Plus Natural Timber

Sage green is having a sustained moment in Australian coastal interiors — and it's earned it. Unlike the saturated greens of the 1990s or the cool eucalyptus greens of recent years, soft sage green sits perfectly between ocean and landscape, warm enough to feel Australian and muted enough to feel sophisticated.

**The specific paints:**

- **Walls (primary):** Dulux Natural White half-strength — keeps the space light
- **Feature wall or bathroom:** Dulux Tranquil Retreat — a soft, muted sage green that works in coastal living rooms, bedrooms, and bathrooms equally. One of Dulux's most consistent coastal performers.
- **Alternative feature colour:** Taubmans Garden Party (a slightly warmer, more yellow-toned sage) — better in rooms with cooler light, where Tranquil Retreat can read blue-grey.
- **Trim:** Dulux Natural White full-strength — a subtle step up from the walls, not stark white

 Sage green works in coastal interiors specifically because it bridges the gap between the ocean and the Australian bush. Most coastal Australian landscapes contain both elements — and sage green is the colour that connects them. It's not a purely maritime colour, which is why it avoids the cliched "beach house" feeling that straight navy or aqua creates.

**Where this palette belongs:**
- Coastal bathroom: Sage green tiles or painted wall, white grout, natural timber vanity
- Master bedroom: Sage green feature wall behind the bed, warm white remaining walls
- Living room: Sage green as cushion and accessory accent only — not on the walls, which can feel heavy

**Key point:** Dulux Tranquil Retreat as a feature wall colour (one wall only, ~$65-$75 AUD for 4L at Bunnings) is the sage green option most suited to Australian coastal interiors without reading dated or oversaturated.

## 4. Coastal Hamptons: Bright White Plus Soft Navy Plus Natural Oak

This is the Australian interpretation of the American Hamptons palette — and the key word is "Australian interpretation." The American version uses cooler, brighter whites and more saturated navy. The version that works here is warmer and more restrained, reflecting the warmer quality of Australian light.



**The specific paints:**

- **Walls:** Dulux Lexicon Half — a crisp, barely warm white that's the closest to a neutral white while still avoiding the clinical quality of cool white. This is the white most commonly used in Australian Hamptons-style homes. Widely available at Bunnings.
- **Trim and shiplap:** Dulux Vivid White — used on architraves, skirting, window frames, and any shiplap or panelling for a clean contrast against the Lexicon Half walls.
- **Feature colour (navy):** Dulux Blue Jeans (a soft, desaturated navy-blue) or Taubmans Midnight Sail (a truer, deeper navy for those wanting more contrast). Use on cabinetry, a single feature wall, or a kitchen island — not on primary room walls.

**Where this palette works best:**
- Open-plan living and dining: Lexicon Half throughout, Vivid White trim, navy in throw cushions and decorative accessories only
- Kitchen: White Vivid cabinetry, navy island or lower cabinetry, Lexicon Half walls
- Master bedroom: Lexicon Half walls, Vivid White cornices and trim, navy or natural linen bedding

**Key point:** Dulux Lexicon Half (walls) paired with Dulux Vivid White (trim) is the most reliably beautiful Australian Hamptons-style paint combination — both are available from Bunnings at ~$65-$75 AUD per 4L.

## 5. Barefoot Beach: Soft Terracotta Plus Warm White Plus Sandy Beige

This is the more unexpected coastal palette — and the one that surprises people most with how well it works in Australian beach house interiors. Terracotta reads as a coastal colour in Australia because it's the colour of sandstone cliffs, red-dirt coastlines, and the terracotta-pot gardens of every coastal home north of the Tropic of Capricorn.

![Coastal living room with warm white walls, a terracotta ceramic vase, rattan furniture, and sandy beige rug](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1586023492125-27b2c045efd7?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

**The specific paints:**

- **Walls (primary):** Dulux Antique White USA — warmer than Natural White, the right foundation for terracotta accents
- **Feature colour:** Dulux Outback Sunset (a muted, warm terracotta) or Taubmans Sunbaked (a truer, more saturated terracotta for bolder statements). Use sparingly — one feature wall, or kitchen lower cabinetry only.
- **Trim:** Dulux Antique White USA — matching the walls rather than contrasting them keeps the space warm and cohesive

**Where terracotta works in a coastal interior:**
- As accessories only (terracotta pots, ceramic vases, woven baskets with terracotta detail) — the safest entry point
- As a feature wall in a bedroom where a warm, cocooning quality is desired
- On cabinetry in a kitchen or bathroom where the surrounding walls remain warm white

**Key point:** Dulux Outback Sunset as a single feature wall (one tin of 4L at ~$65-$75 AUD covers approximately 10m2) introduces terracotta warmth to a coastal interior without overwhelming the space.

## 6. Deep Coastal: Soft Navy Feature Wall Plus Warm White Plus Natural Timber

Used correctly, a deep navy feature wall is one of the most dramatic and beautiful things you can do in a coastal Australian interior. Used incorrectly, it makes a room feel like a corporate hotel bathroom. The difference is entirely in the other palette elements and the scale of the navy application.

**The rules for coastal navy:**
- One wall only — the wall behind the bed in a master bedroom, or the wall behind the sofa in a living room
- All other walls must be warm white (Dulux Natural White or Lexicon Half) to balance the weight of the navy
- All furniture and textiles must remain in natural tones (white linen, jute, natural timber) — no other saturated colours
- The navy must be muted, not bright — Dulux Blue Jeans or Dulux Hog Bristle Navy, not Dulux Deep Ocean

**The specific paints:**

- **Feature wall:** Dulux Blue Jeans — a soft, desaturated navy that reads calm rather than heavy. Alternatively, Taubmans Midnight Blue for a deeper, more saturated statement. Both available at Bunnings.
- **Remaining walls:** Dulux Lexicon Half or Natural White half-strength — essential to keep the room from feeling dark
- **Trim:** Dulux Vivid White — a clean contrast that defines the navy feature without competing with it

**Key point:** Dulux Blue Jeans on a single feature wall (~$65-$75 AUD for 4L at Bunnings) is the most consistently beautiful deep coastal paint choice — muted enough to feel sophisticated, deep enough to make a statement.

## 7. Muted Aqua: Soft Aqua-Green Plus Warm White Plus Bleached Timber

Aqua is the colour Australian coastal interiors overuse most reliably — and then abandon a season later because it aged poorly. The mistake is saturation. Bright, swimming-pool aqua is a children's bedroom colour. Muted, slightly grey aqua is a genuinely sophisticated coastal accent.

**The specific paints:**

- **Feature or accent wall:** Dulux Soft Sage (a warm, slightly aqua-tinted green) or Taubmans Ocean Haze (a genuine soft aqua-grey). Both are muted enough to age beautifully and saturated enough to read as a clear colour choice.
- **Walls (primary):** Dulux Natural White half-strength throughout — the warm white anchor that keeps the aqua from reading clinical
- **Trim:** Dulux Natural White full-strength

**Where muted aqua works:**
- Coastal bathroom: Aqua subway tiles with white grout and a white vanity
- Bedroom: Aqua cushions and accessories against warm white walls — not on the walls themselves
- Kitchen splashback: Aqua zellige or subway tiles behind a white kitchen

 In a renovation project completed on the Sunshine Coast in 2025, a master bathroom repainted from clinical white to Taubmans Ocean Haze, with a new timber vanity and natural fibre mat, was cited by the selling agent as the single renovation change most mentioned by buyers during open inspections — at a total paint cost of under $150 AUD.

**Key point:** Taubmans Ocean Haze in a bathroom or as a bedroom accent wall (~$60-$70 AUD for 4L at Bunnings) is the muted aqua choice that ages best in Australian coastal interiors.

## 8. Coastal Exterior: Weathered White Plus Coastal Sage Plus Warm Timber Decking

Exterior colour in a coastal Australian environment is both a design decision and a practical one. Salt air, intense UV, and the occasional tropical storm degrade standard exterior paint significantly faster than in inland environments — which means both the paint product and the colour choice matter.

![Australian beach house exterior with weathered white painted weatherboards, coastal sage window trim, and natural timber deck](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1600596542815-ffad4c1539a9?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

**Paint products for coastal exteriors:**
All exterior paint in coastal locations should come from a specifically formulated exterior range. Both Dulux Weathershield and Taubmans All Weather exterior ranges are engineered for Australian coastal conditions — UV resistance, flexibility to handle expansion and contraction, and mould resistance. Both are available from Bunnings.

**The exterior palette:**

- **Primary exterior:** Dulux Lexicon Half exterior formula — a crisp, barely warm white that's the most widely used beach house exterior colour in Australia. Alternatively, Dulux Natural White exterior formula for a warmer, more weatherboard-cottage feel.
- **Window and door trim:** Dulux Vivid White exterior — creates a clean contrast against the off-white body colour, defining the architecture
- **Feature trim or shutters:** Dulux Tranquil Retreat exterior (sage green) or Taubmans Midnight Sail exterior (navy) — both create beautiful contrast accents on a white beach house exterior
- **Decking:** Dulux Weathershield Decking Oil in Natural Cedar or Jarrah — protects and enriches the natural timber colour without going too dark or too orange

**Key point:** Dulux Lexicon Half exterior formula from Bunnings (~$120-$180 AUD per 10L) is the most consistently beautiful and durable Australian beach house exterior colour — pair with Vivid White trim for a clean, classic coastal result.

## 9. The Warm Minimal: Off-White Plus Warm Grey Plus Pale Stone

This is the coastal palette for Australian homeowners who want the light, airy quality of a coastal interior without any explicit "beach house" signifiers. No navy. No rattan (unless it's very restrained). No aqua. Just a beautifully calibrated warm minimal palette that happens to suit the coastal light perfectly.

**The specific paints:**

- **Walls:** Dulux Whisper White — a soft, barely perceptible warm white that reads as neutral in almost any light. The most understated of the white options here.
- **Feature or accent:** Dulux Monument (a deep warm grey, use sparingly on a single architectural element) or Dulux Hog Bristle (a softer warm grey for walls in a lower-light room)
- **Trim:** Dulux Vivid White or Natural White full-strength

**Furniture palette that matches:**
- Light stone or concrete surfaces (kitchen benchtop, coffee table)
- Natural pale oak timber
- White or warm grey linen
- Matte black or brushed brass metal as a single accent (light fittings, tapware)

**Where this palette works:**
- Contemporary coastal apartments in Sydney or Melbourne, where the view does the "coastal" work and the interior can remain restrained
- Renovated beach houses where the original architecture is modern or low-ceiling — this palette adds light without competing with the architecture

**Key point:** Dulux Whisper White walls with Dulux Hog Bristle as a warm grey accent (~$65-$75 AUD per 4L at Bunnings) creates a contemporary coastal interior that doesn't rely on any explicit beachy styling to feel right.

---

The Australian coastal colour palette is not a single look — it's a set of principles. Warm undertones over cool. Natural references over synthetic ones. Restraint with the accent colours that photograph beautifully in a showroom but age poorly in a real home. Get the foundation white right, choose one accent colour and apply it sparingly, and trust the natural materials — timber, rattan, linen, jute — to do most of the visual work.
